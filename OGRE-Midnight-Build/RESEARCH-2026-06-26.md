## CROSS-CUTTING THEMES — WHAT THE GLOBAL PICTURE TELLS US

### 1. The Sovereign AI Race is On — and Africa is the Last Prize
The US (G7 coalition, $295B+ commitments), China ($295B national plan), Japan ($19B + SoftBank 1GW data center), and India ($2B+ National AI Mission) are all building state-backed AI infrastructure. Africa has zero hyperscaler-owned data centers at frontier scale, per the 2026 AI Index. This is not a gap — it is a **once-in-a-generation opportunity** for African-owned sovereign AI infrastructure. Every month of inaction by African enterprises is a month that gap closes as Big Tech builds directly into Africa.

### 2. AI Pricing is Collapsing — Margins Are in Infrastructure and Compliance
Preferred Networks priced their AI at less than half OpenAI's cost. Krutrim Cloud is undercutting US hyperscalers for Global South workloads. China's open-weight models (Qwen, DeepSeek) are free or near-free. The inference cost curve is collapsing so fast that **the money in AI is no longer in the models — it's in the infrastructure wrapping them, the domain fine-tuning on top, and the compliance/governance layer enterprises demand**.

### 3. Financial Inclusion AI is Proven — Africa's 1.2B Unbanked Are Next
Nubank's $8.2B AI bet has proven that AI-driven credit scoring works at emerging-market scale. The Brazilian playbook (alternative data + AI + mobile-first distribution) is directly applicable to Africa's 1.2B+ unbanked population. No major African fintech has yet deployed an equivalent at scale — this is the single biggest AI opportunity on the continent.

### 4. Healthcare AI is Africa's Highest-Impact Entry Point
Discovery Health's Vitality AI (2.7M+ members), South Africa's rural healthcare AI pilots (June 2026 TV feature), AMLD Africa 2026 in Johannesburg — all point to healthcare AI as Africa's most immediate high-impact use case. AI-driven triage, diagnostic assistance, drug adherence, and health pathway management in under-resourced African health systems will save millions of lives and generate enormous data flywheels.

### 5. Policy Windows Close Fast
South Africa's Draft National AI Policy public comment period, the EU AI Act's African trade implications, and America's G7 coalition standards — all are being set in 2026. Enterprises and organizations that engage with these processes now will shape the rules. Those that wait will simply have to obey them.
