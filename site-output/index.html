// MEET MAPIRI MONAMA

The Girl Who Means Business

12 years old. Grade 7 at Crowland Prep, Johannesburg. One dream: to be just like her mother — a successful businesswoman.

👧🏾

Maphiri Monama

Grade 7 · Crowland Prep

Age 12 · Entrepreneur

The ambitious, the fearless, the brilliant.

Fierce Ambitious Resilient Courageous Driven Innocent

Maphiri dreams bigger than her circumstances. With best friend Thembi at her side and a crush on John that complicates everything beautifully, she sets her sights on the Grade 7 Market Day — and the world of entrepreneurship — with more courage than most adults have in a lifetime.

Her journey through Market Day Dreams and the Valentine Venture teaches her that business is not just about money — it's about resilience, creativity, and believing in yourself.

  • Best friend: Thembi — always in on the plan
  • Crush: John — complicates everything beautifully
  • Hero: Her mother — the businesswoman she wants to become
  • School: Crowland Prep, Johannesburg
  • Grade: 7 (Senior Primary)

// ABOUT THE AUTHOR

Meet Keamogetswe Matsho

👩🏾‍💻

Keamogetswe Matsho

Woolworths SA Youth Maker 2026

Keamogetswe Matsho

Author · Blogger · Businesswoman · Founder, Maphiri Foundation

Keamogetswe Matsho is the author of Maphiri's Marvellous Money Moves — a groundbreaking children's novel bringing financial literacy and entrepreneurship to life through the adventures of a 12-year-old South African girl.

Named one of Woolworths SA's 2026 Youth Makers, Keamogetswe is on a mission to equip young people across Africa with the financial knowledge and entrepreneurial spirit they need to build their futures.

Through Maphiri's story, she shows children that entrepreneurship is not just for adults — it starts with an idea, a plan, and the courage to try.
