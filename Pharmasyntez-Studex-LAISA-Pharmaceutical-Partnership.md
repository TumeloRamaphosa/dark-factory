# PART 2 — REGULATORY PATHWAY (SAHPRA)

## 2.1 What Licenses Are Required

To distribute pharmaceutical products in South Africa, the following are required under the **South African Health Products Regulatory Authority (SAHPRA)**:

| License Type | Requirement | Cost |
|-------------|-------------|------|
| **Wholesale Dealer License** | Required to store and distribute medicines | R15,000 per license |
| **Importer License** | Required to bring products into SA | Included in wholesaler license |
| **Manufacturer License** | If repackaging or manufacturing locally | R25,200 |
| **Distribution License** | For distribution without storage | R15,000 |
| **FAMC (Fixed Appliance, Medicine Cabinet)** | For retail pharmacy level | Additional |

**LAISA's existing medical infrastructure provides a foundation** — LAISA's licenses as a registered medical/aesthetic clinic create a regulatory compliance foundation that can be extended to pharmaceutical distribution under the Studex Global Markets umbrella.

## 2.2 Registration Requirements for Russian Products

| Step | Requirement | Timeline |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **1. Product Registration** | Each formulation must be registered with SAHPRA | 12-18 months per product |
| **2. GMP Recognition** | SAHPRA recognises GMP certificates from PIC/S member countries (Russia is not yet a full PIC/S member — requires additional verification) | 3-6 months |
| **3. Import Permit** | Permit required per consignment | 2-4 weeks |
| **4. Lab Testing** | Batch testing of imported products at SAHPRA-approved labs | 1-2 months |
| **5. Cold Chain Compliance** | Temperature-controlled storage compliance for thermolabile products (insulin, vaccines, biologics) | Infrastructure required |

**Accelerated pathway:** Generic medicines already registered in Stringent Regulatory Authority (SRA) countries can qualify for SAHPRA's **abbreviated review pathway**, potentially reducing registration time to **6-9 months**.
