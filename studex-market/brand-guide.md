# StudEx Super Agents — Brand Guidelines
## Pixel Noir. Afro-Futurist. Built for Africa.

---

## 1. Brand Essence

StudEx is the brand that thinks the future of African business is automated, affordable, and accessible. We are pixel-art bold in a world of corporate blue SaaS. We speak WhatsApp in a world of enterprise dashboards. We charge R83/day in a world where "contact us for pricing" is a barrier.

Our brand essence is **Afro-Futurist Utility** — the aesthetic of tomorrow, the function of today. Not sci-fi escapism. Not retro nostalgia. The pixel-art future that African entrepreneurs actually live in: dark screens glowing in dimly-lit offices, neon accents on township streets, WhatsApp green cutting through the dark.

**For African SMB owners who are running their businesses on WhatsApp and can't afford enterprise AI tools, StudEx Super Agents is the AI agent platform that meets them where they work — at R83/day — because no one else built it for them.**

## 3. The Pixel Noir Theme

### What Pixel Noir Means

Pixel Noir is the visual and emotional world of StudEx. It is:

**Pixel** — The aesthetic of digital construction. The grid. The building blocks. Every pixel placed with intention, like every task an agent handles. This is also a nod to the developer/builder community: the people who understand that complexity is built from simple elements.

**Noir** — Dark. Serious. Not corporate-serious, but noir-film serious. This is a world where business happens at all hours, in dark offices lit by screen glow, on WhatsApp screens bright in dark rooms. The noir aesthetic says: this is not a toy. This is not a demo. This is a tool that works in the real world.

**Combined** — Pixel Noir is the visual language of an African tech company that didn't try to look like Silicon Valley. It's the aesthetic that emerges when you build for your own market instead of copying someone else's.

### When to Use Pixel Noir

- Every touchpoint where the brand appears: website, social media, pitch decks, invoices, email signatures
- App UI: dark mode primary, pixel-grid backgrounds, neon accent highlights
- Photography: dark environments, single-source dramatic lighting, contrast-heavy
- Video: dark studio setups, glowing UI elements, screen-share overlays with neon annotation
- Print: event banners, business cards, merchandise

### When NOT to Use Pixel Noir

- Client-facing documents where they expect a corporate feel (formal proposals, contracts)
- Markets where the full brand identity hasn't been established yet — lead with the name and colour, not the full pixel art system

## 4. Color Palette

### Primary Brand Colors

**StudEx Black (Background)**
```
Hex: #0A0A0F
```
The primary background for all digital surfaces. Use for website backgrounds, app dark mode, presentation backgrounds. Never use pure black (#000000) — #0A0A0F has a slight warmth that reads better on screens and reduces eye strain in dark environments.

**StudEx White (Text on Dark)**
```
Hex: #F0F0F5
```
Primary text color on dark backgrounds. Slightly off-white — pure white (#FFFFFF) is too harsh against the deep black. Use for body text, headings on dark backgrounds.

**StudEx Neon Green (Primary Accent — WhatsApp Reference)**
```
Hex: #00E676
```
The brand's action color. Buttons, links, highlights, status indicators, success states. Named deliberately: this is the green of WhatsApp — the channel that powers African business. Use this color to signal: this is interactive, this is active, this is the right choice.

**StudEx Gold (Secondary Accent — Premium)**
```
Hex: #F5A623
```
Used sparingly for premium indicators, pricing highlights, achievement badges, and "most popular" labels. Contrasts beautifully against the dark background. Use for: pricing tier highlights, "recommended" badges, referral program elements.

### Supporting Colors

**StudEx Gray (Subdued Text / Secondary UI)**
```
Hex: #4A4A5A
```
Body text on light backgrounds, secondary UI elements, borders, dividers. Use when #F0F0F5 would be too prominent — for metadata, timestamps, helper text.

**StudEx Dark Gray (Cards / Elevated Surfaces)**
```
Hex: #14141F
```
Elevated card backgrounds, modal overlays, sidebar panels. One shade lighter than the primary background — creates visual hierarchy without leaving the dark theme.

**StudEx Red (Error / Warning)**
```
Hex: #FF5252
```
Error states, validation failures, destructive actions. Use consistently so users learn the signal.

**StudEx Blue (Informational / Links)**
```
Hex: #448AFF
```
Informational callouts, secondary links, neutral notifications. Not as prominent as Neon Green — this is for less urgent actions.

**StudEx Purple (AI / Intelligence Signal)**
```
Hex: #7C4DFF
```
Used exclusively for AI-related visual cues: the AI typing indicator, "thinking" states, intelligence features. This differentiates AI activity visually from general system activity.

### Color Usage Rules

1. **Background first.** Always start with #0A0A0F as the canvas.
2. **Text second.** Use #F0F0F5 for primary, #4A4A5A for secondary.
3. **Accent sparingly.** Neon Green (#00E676) should cover no more than 5% of any screen. Too much green kills the signal.
4. **Never use full saturation.** The accent colors above are already at effective saturation for screens. Do not intensify them further.
5. **Test on mobile first.** Always check color legibility on a dark-mode phone screen. Colors that look good on a MacBook can be unreadable on a budget Android device.

## 5. Typography

### Font Stack

**Headlines (H1, H2): Orbitron**

```
Font: Orbitron
Weight: 700 (Bold) for H1, 600 (SemiBold) for H2
Fallback: 'Courier New', monospace
URL: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Orbitron
```

Orbitron is the font of screens, of futures, of interfaces. It is angular, geometric, and unmistakably digital. It says: this is technology, built for the future.

Use Orbitron for:
- H1 (hero headlines): 48px–64px, tracking +0.05em
- H2 (section headings): 32px–40px, tracking +0.03em
- Feature card titles: 20px–24px
- Logo text

**Body Text (All body copy): JetBrains Mono**

```
Font: JetBrains Mono
Weight: 400 (Regular) for body, 500 (Medium) for emphasis
Fallback: 'Courier New', monospace
URL: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/JetBrains+Mono
```

JetBrains Mono is the coder's font — legible, distinctive, technical without being cold. It reinforces the "agent" and "automation" narrative: this is a tool built by people who understand how work actually gets done.

Use JetBrains Mono for:
- All body text: 16px, line-height 1.6
- Feature descriptions: 15px
- Button labels: 14px, weight 500, uppercase, tracking +0.1em
- Code snippets, agent output displays, Command Center data

**Subheadings / Labels (Supporting text): Rajdhani**

```
Font: Rajdhani
Weight: 500 (Medium), 600 (SemiBold)
Fallback: 'Arial Narrow', sans-serif
URL: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Rajdhani
```

Rajdhani is the connecting font — angular enough to feel related to Orbitron, but more readable at small sizes. It's the font of labels, captions, navigation items, and supporting copy.

Use Rajdhani for:
- Navigation labels: 14px, tracking +0.05em
- Subheadings (H3, H4): 18px–22px
- Caption text: 12px–13px
- Form labels: 14px, uppercase

## 6. Logo System

### The StudEx Claw (Primary Logo Mark)

The StudEx Claw is the bear claw icon — a four-toed claw with pixel-art geometry. It is the primary brand identifier and must appear on all materials.

**Logo construction:**
- Pixel-art geometric style: squares and rectangles only, no curves
- Four toes, arched upper profile
- The negative space inside the claw forms a subtle ">" chevron pointing right — directional, forward, progress
- Neon Green (#00E676) fill on Black (#0A0A0F) background

**Minimum sizes:**
- Digital: minimum 24px height for icon-only use
- Print: minimum 10mm height for icon-only use
- Always maintain clear space equal to the height of one claw toe around all sides

**Never do these things with the Claw:**
- Stretch or distort the proportions
- Rotate to non-90-degree angles
- Apply gradients to the claw shape
- Place on a background lighter than #1A1A2A (must have sufficient contrast)
- Use the claw in body text or inline (it's a logo mark, not a bullet point)

### The Text Mark

**StudEx** — Orbitron 700, tracking +0.1em, in #F0F0F5

**Super Agents** — Rajdhani 600, tracking +0.15em, in #00E676 (only when used as a full brand line, not on navigation)

### Logo Lockups

**Horizontal (Primary — website header, pitch decks):**
StudEx Claw + "StudEx" text mark + "Super Agents" in accent green. Standard spacing: claw width = gap = text.

**Stacked (Social media profile picture, app icon):**
StudEx Claw centered, with "StudEx" below in Orbitron 700 at 60% of claw height.

**Icon Only (Favicon, app icon, merchandise):**
StudEx Claw only. No text. Pixel-perfect at 32px×32px and 16px×16px.

## 7. Image Style

### The Hybrid Aesthetic

StudEx visual content is a deliberate hybrid of two worlds:

**Pixel Art Elements:**
- Pixel-art icons and illustrations used in feature cards, empty states, and on-page graphics
- 8-bit and 16-bit style illustrations of African cityscapes (Lagos skyline, Johannesburg panorama)
- Pixel-art representations of the agent as a character — a glowing, friendly claw with a "face" made of pixel squares
- UI wireframe elements in pixel-art style

**Real Photography:**
- African entrepreneurs in their real working environments: a Lagos market stall with a phone, a Johannesburg salon floor, a Nairobi logistics dispatch office
- Dark, high-contrast photography: shallow depth of field, single light source, moody
- No staged stock photos of people in business casual shaking hands
- If showing a screen in a photo, the screen should be showing the Pixel Command Center or WhatsApp — never blank or showing irrelevant content

### Photography Guidelines

**Do:**
- Show hands on phones — WhatsApp is a hand-held medium
- Show faces in environments — authentic, not airbrushed
- Use African cities as backdrops — identifiable landmarks, township textures, market colours
- Show the pixel art overlaid on photography — the agent as a visual character in real scenes
- Dark, cinematic colour grading — oranges and teals from Lagos street photography

**Don't:**
- Show stock photography of Western offices or boardrooms
- Show posed "business meeting" scenarios
- Use bright, flat, corporate lighting
- Show people looking directly at camera with fake smiles
- Use abstract technology imagery (glowing circuits, data streams) — too generic

### Image Sourcing

- **Self-generated:** The founder's team should be taking real photos from day one. Every client onboarding session is a photography opportunity.
- **Illustrations:** Commission pixel-art illustrations of African business scenarios from Fiverr or local African illustrators (search "pixel art Africa" on Fiverr or Instagram).
- **Stock:** Use Unsplash for generic business contexts (warehouse, street market, office) — never for people faces. Never use Getty or iStock — the aesthetic is wrong.

## 8. Icon Style

### The Rule: Pixel-Art Inspired Line Icons

All icons used in the StudEx product, website, and marketing materials follow these rules:

**Line-based:** Stroke icons, not filled icons. 1.5px–2px stroke weight. Consistent stroke width across all icons in a set.

**Pixel grid alignment:** Stroke endpoints should align to a virtual 4px or 8px grid. Where a stroke would naturally end between grid lines, extend to the nearest grid line — this is what creates the pixel-art feel.

**Rounded joins:** Stroke-linejoin: round. Stroke-linecap: round. This keeps the icons from feeling harsh while maintaining the line quality.

**Consistent sizing:** All icons rendered at 24×24px as a base. Scaling should be in multiples (24, 48, 72, 96).

**Color:** Always #00E676 for active/enabled icons. #4A4A5A for inactive/secondary.

**Examples of correct pixel-art line icons:**
- WhatsApp icon: Pixel grid version of the WhatsApp logo — blocky, pixel-aligned rather than the smooth official version
- Clock icon: Square face, pixel-aligned tick marks
- Lightning bolt (automation): Stepped pixel edges rather than smooth curves
- Bear claw (brand): The StudEx Claw itself — as an icon, not just a logo
- Chart icon: Bar chart with pixel-block bars, not smooth gradient bars
- Message bubble: Square-cornered speech bubble with pixel dots inside

### Icon Resources

- **Custom:** Design in Figma using the pixel grid plugin. Export as SVG with consistent stroke settings.
- **Reference library:** Phosphor Icons (line style) as a reference — but adapt to pixel-art aesthetic by adjusting to 4px grid alignment.
- **Never use:** Font Awesome (too corporate), Material Icons (too generic), Emoji (inconsistent across platforms).

## 9. Tone of Voice

### The Voice in 5 Words

**Bold. Direct. Afro-Futurist. Transparent. Human.**

**Bold:** We say what others won't. "Cheaper than a minimum wage employee." "Works while you sleep." "Built for Africa, not adapted for Africa." We don't hedge.

**Direct:** We don't use 20 words when 5 will do. We don't use "leverage synergies" when we mean "use better." Every sentence earns its place.

**Afro-Futurist:** We speak from an African context, for African businesses. Our references are Lagos, Johannesburg, Nairobi. Our currency is R and ₦. Our language is WhatsApp.

**Transparent:** Pricing is on the website. Limits are named. We say what we can do and what we can't. We don't do dark patterns.

**Human:** We're a team of Africans building for Africans. We use contractions, we use humor, we respond to our customers by name. We're not a faceless SaaS company. We're Tumelo and the team, and we know your business because it's our business too.

## 10. Copy Examples: On-Brand vs Off-Brand

### Example 1: Hero Headline

**On-brand (✅):**
"Your Second Employee is an Agent."

**Off-brand (❌):**
"Revolutionizing African SMB Operations Through Next-Generation Agentic AI Solutions" — Too corporate, too buzzword-heavy, wrong voice.

**Off-brand (❌):**
"AI-Powered WhatsApp Automation for Modern African Businesses" — Accurate but flat. Describes, doesn't inspire.

---

### Example 2: Feature Description

**On-brand (✅):**
"Your agent answers WhatsApp messages at 11 PM, sends quotes with photos, and follows up on leads you forgot existed. While you sleep."

**Off-brand (❌):**
"Our intelligent multi-channel communication agent leverages natural language processing to deliver contextual customer responses across WhatsApp Business API integration." — No one talks like this. No one reads this.

---

### Example 3: Pricing CTA

**On-brand (✅):**
"R83/day. Less than your morning coffee. Less than a minimum wage employee. More than a chatbot."

**Off-brand (❌):**
"Starting from R1,500/month with tier-based pricing and volume discounts available upon request." — Bureaucratic. Hidden. Afraid to state the price simply.

---

### Example 4: Objection Handling (AI Trust)

**On-brand (✅):**
"Look, I get it — you've tried 'AI' before and it was a glorified auto-responder that embarrassed you in front of customers. Our agents are different because you train them with your own business data, you set the rules, and you can step in at any moment. It's a tool that works for you, not instead of you."

**Off-brand (❌):**
"StudEx leverages advanced machine learning algorithms trained on billions of data points to deliver enterprise-grade AI interactions with full explainability and audit compliance." — Wrong register for the audience.

---

### Example 5: Testimonial Introduction

**On-brand (✅):**

## 12. Brand Do's and Don'ts

### Always Do

✅ Write like a person who has run a business in Africa — specific, grounded, honest
✅ Use R and ₦ currency, not USD, unless specifically addressing diaspora
✅ Reference WhatsApp as the channel — it's the most natural word in the brand vocabulary
✅ Use numbers as weapons — R83/day, R2.77/hour, 847 messages, 30% more orders
✅ Show the Command Center — visibility is credibility
✅ Be proud of the pricing — it's the brand's superpower
✅ Use African city names — Lagos, Johannesburg, Nairobi are brand assets
✅ Write copy that reads like a WhatsApp message from a friend — conversational, direct, warm

### Never Do

❌ Use "disruptive," "game-changing," "leveraging synergies" — kill words
❌ Compare to Microsoft/Google/Salesforce without a specific, credible point
❌ Use stock photography of non-African offices or generic business scenarios
❌ Write copy that sounds like it came from an enterprise SaaS company
❌ Hide pricing behind "contact us" or "book a demo to learn more"
❌ Use pure white (#FFFFFF) or pure black (#000000) as primary colors
❌ Apply gradient backgrounds to large areas — the dark theme needs flat color
❌ Make the brand about "AI" as a concept — make it about what the AI *does*
❌ Write long paragraphs — maximum 2-3 sentences per paragraph on web copy
❌ Use emoji excessively — one or two maximum per post/message. The claw emoji 🐾 is the brand's emoji. Use it instead of generic emoji where possible.
