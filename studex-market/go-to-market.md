# StudEx Super Agents — 60-Day Go-To-Market Plan
## Launch Strategy for the African SMB Market

---

## Executive Summary

This plan outlines the launch of StudEx Super Agents across South Africa, Nigeria, and Kenya over 60 days, targeting R0 to R180,000 MRR by Day 60. The strategy is deliberately lean: no paid ads in the first month, no external agency, no big launch event. Instead, it relies on personal networks, strategic content, and the single most credible marketing channel for African SMBs — word of mouth from real customers.

**Phase 1 (Days 1-14): Infrastructure & Waitlist**
**Phase 2 (Days 15-30): Soft Launch — First 10 Clients**
**Phase 3 (Days 31-60): Referral Engine + Content + PR**

## Phase 2: Soft Launch — First 10 Clients (Days 15-30)

### Strategy: The Founder's Network Is the Launchpad

In African SMB markets, a personal referral from someone you trust is worth more than any advertising dollar. The soft launch is designed to turn Tumelo's network into 10 satisfied, vocal clients who become the first cohort of referrers.

### Pricing for First 10 Clients

**Intro Price: R1,500/month (60% off standard Growth price of R3,800)**

Why this price:
- It's the minimum viable revenue that validates the business model
- It's not free — free clients don't value the product
- At R1,500, clients have skin in the game, which drives real usage
- It sets the expectation for future pricing at R3,800 — clients who convert to full price are upsells

**The deal terms:**
- 60-day intro period at R1,500/month
- At day 60, auto-escalate to R3,800/month (Growth plan)
- Client must commit to one onboarding call and one 30-day review call
- Client agrees to provide a testimonial and/or case study data at day 30

### Client Selection Criteria

Prioritize clients who:
1. Have high WhatsApp enquiry volume (20+ messages/day from customers)
2. Are already paying for some form of admin support or virtual assistant
3. Have the founder's personal trust backing (warm referrals only — no cold acquisition during soft launch)
4. Represent a vertical we want case studies for

**Target verticals for first 10 clients:**
- 3x E-commerce / online retail
- 2x Beauty salons / spas
- 2x Construction / trade services
- 2x Logistics / delivery businesses
- 1x Professional services / consultancy

### Onboarding the First 10

**The Onboarding Call (30 minutes)**
Agenda:
1. (5 min) Understand the business: what does a typical day of WhatsApp look like?
2. (10 min) Configure the agent: pricing, FAQ responses, tone settings
3. (10 min) Connect WhatsApp, run a live test with a simulated customer message
4. (5 min) Set expectations: what the agent will handle, what it will flag, when to intervene

**Day 3 Check-In:** WhatsApp message from the account manager: "How's Day 1 going? Anything the agent needs to learn?"

**Day 7 Review:** 10-minute call to review Command Center data and adjust agent behaviour.

**Day 30 Review:** Full 30-day retrospective — review metrics, collect testimonial, confirm upgrade to R3,800/month or churn.

### Conversion Mechanics

At Day 28, send the Day 13 email from the email sequence (trial reminder) — adapted for the soft launch context: "Your intro period ends in 30 days. Here's what your agent handled this month. [Command Center screenshot.] Ready to continue at R3,800/month? Here's what you'll get for R83/day more..."

## Phase 3: Referral Engine + Scale (Days 31-60)

### Referral Program Launch

**Mechanics:**
- Every client who refers a new paying customer who completes their first month receives R250 credit
- Credit applies to their next invoice — not cash, not a payout
- Maximum 5 referrals credited per client per month (prevents abuse)
- Referral link is unique per client and tracked in Command Center
- Referral credit auto-applies when referee hits day 30

**Why this works for African SMBs:**
- R250 credit is meaningful — it's nearly 1 week of service at Growth pricing
- African entrepreneurs talk to each other constantly — WhatsApp groups, industry groups, church, school
- A satisfied client has more credibility in their network than any ad
- The referral program makes the satisfied client an active promoter with a financial incentive

## Partnership Strategy

### Target: Business Incubators & Accelerators (SA Priority)

African business incubators and accelerators are the highest-density concentration of StudEx ICPs — early-stage to growth-stage entrepreneurs who are actively building and need operational leverage.

**Targets (Days 31-60):**

1. **Raizcorp** (South Africa) — 20-year-old business incubator with 500+ alumni. Monthly founder events. Partnership angle: StudEx as a member benefit/perk.
2. **The B-BBEE Technology Transfer Partnership (SA)** — Connection to township and SMME development ecosystem. Potential PR value.
3. **MEST Africa** (Pan-African, Ghana-based) — Has presence in Nigeria and Kenya. Founder network is exactly the StudEx ICP.

**Partnership Offer:**

"We offer your members a 60-day free trial (extended from our standard 14-day) + a dedicated onboarding session + 20% discount on Growth plan for 12 months if they sign up during the offer period."

**Value for the incubator:** Credible tech tool as a member benefit. Demonstrates they're equipping founders with modern tools. No financial cost to the incubator.

**Value for StudEx:** Access to warm cohort of entrepreneurs who are actively building and networking with each other — a built-in referral network.

### Target: WhatsApp Business Solution Providers

African businesses that help other businesses set up WhatsApp Business accounts (agencies, consultants, integrators) are natural distribution partners. Approach 5 WhatsApp Business-focused agencies in SA/Nigeria to offer white-label/reseller partnerships.

### What This Budget Cannot Buy (And Why That's Fine)

It cannot buy paid advertising. It cannot buy a PR agency. It cannot buy a content team. This is intentional.

The African SMB market does not trust ads. It trusts founders, it trusts peers, and it trusts results. Every rand spent should go toward either: (a) making the product better for early clients, or (b) getting the product in front of early clients through trusted channels.

The 60-day plan is designed to generate enough MRR (R75,000 by Day 60) to fund the next phase organically — and to build the social proof that makes paid acquisition viable at Day 90+.

## Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| WhatsApp API restrictions or rate limiting | Medium | Multi-channel backup (SMS, email) ready in Growth plan. MobileMonkey or similar as fallback. |
| Early clients churn after intro pricing ends | Medium | Intensive Day 28 retention call. Show Command Center ROI data. Anchor to time-saved calculation. |
| Onboarding is too time-intensive for team | High | Document the onboarding process in detail. Create self-serve onboarding for Starter plan by Week 8. |
| Nigeria/Kenya payment processing delays | Medium | Integrate Paystack (Nigeria) and M-Pesa (Kenya) early. Do not rely on Stripe alone. |
| Competitor launches WhatsApp-native AI agent | Low (60-day window) | First-mover advantage in brand recognition and case studies. Network effects from referrals. |
| Cold outreach low response rate | Medium | Personalize every message. Lead with specific observation about recipient's business. No templates that sound like templates. |
| Founder burnout (Tumelo doing everything) | High | By Week 5, hire one part-time customer success person. Prioritize: handle onboarding calls so Tumelo can focus on content/PR/partnerships. |
