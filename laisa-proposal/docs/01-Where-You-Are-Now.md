# Where You Are Now

> *You built two practices that patients trust with their vision and their confidence. That trust deserves systems that match the quality of care you deliver. This document is an honest look at where those systems stand today — not to criticise, but to make the case for what's possible.*

---

## Your Patient Journeys — Step by Step

### SafeSight Eye Surgery Journey

| Step | What Happens | Time Per Patient | Pain Point |
|------|-------------|:----------------:|------------|
| 1. Discovery | Patient finds you through referrals, word of mouth, or social media | — | No online booking. No search visibility strategy. Patients who find you after hours have no way to take the next step. |
| 2. Enquiry | Patient calls or sends a WhatsApp message to reception | 5–10 min | Reception handles every enquiry manually. During peak hours, calls go unanswered. After hours, enquiries sit until morning. |
| 3. Booking | Reception manually checks availability, confirms, and enters the appointment | 10–15 min | Triple entry: once in Elixir Live, once in My Appointment, once on paper. Typos and double-bookings happen weekly. |
| 4. Confirmation | Reception sends an SMS confirmation and makes a courtesy call the day before | 3–5 min per patient | Entirely manual. If a staff member is busy or away, confirmations get skipped. No automated tracking of who was reminded. |
| 5. Arrival & Intake | Patient arrives, fills out paper consultation and consent forms | 10–15 min per patient | Paper forms slow down check-in. Handwriting can be unclear. Data must later be manually typed into records. |
| 6. Diagnostics | Eye examinations and scans performed | 20–45 min | Clinical — this is where your doctors shine. No pain point here. |
| 7. Consultation | Doctor diagnoses, prescribes, recommends surgery or treatment | 15–30 min | Doctor often walks patient to reception to book follow-up — time that could be spent with the next patient. |
| 8. Follow-Up Booking | Reception books the procedure or follow-up date | 5–10 min | Same manual process as step 3. If the patient needs to check their schedule, reception waits while the phone line backs up. |
| 9. Post-Operative Care | Follow-up appointments and monitoring | Ongoing | Follow-up is ad hoc. If a patient doesn't book their post-op visit at checkout, it falls through the cracks. No automated recall system exists. |

**Total administrative time per eye patient: 40–65 minutes** — of which roughly 25–40 minutes is spent on tasks that should happen automatically.

## Your Current Systems — What Works, What's Missing

| System | Tool | Status | Gap |
|--------|------|--------|-----|
| **Booking** | Elixir Live, My Appointment | Working, but siloed | Two separate systems that don't talk to each other. Data entered twice. |
| **Patient Records** | Google Drive + paper filing | Partial, risky | No search, no backup redundancy, no POPIA compliance structure. Paper files can be lost, damaged, or misfiled. |
| **Billing** | Elixir Live, My Appointment, Sage Accounting | Working, triple-entry | Same invoice entered in three places. Reconciliation takes hours. Medical aid queries require phone calls that take 15–20 minutes each. |
| **CRM** | None | Missing | You have no way to track patient lifecycles, recall patients for follow-ups, or identify your highest-value patients. |
| **Communication** | WhatsApp, Telephone, Email | Fragmented | Different staff handle different channels. Messages get lost. No central record of patient communication. |
| **Marketing** | Instagram, Facebook, TikTok (manual) | Ad hoc | Content created from scratch daily. No scheduling. No strategy. No measurement of what works. |
| **Website** | WordPress | Live, static | Doesn't accept bookings. No live chat. No patient portal. Not working to attract and convert new patients. |
| **Email Marketing** | None | Missing | No way to nurture enquiries, announce promotions, or recall lapsed patients. |
| **Online Advertising** | None | Missing | No Google Ads, no targeted social ads. Patients searching for your services find your competitors first. |
| **System Integration** | None | Critical gap | Every system is a silo. No data flows between booking, billing, records, and communication. Your team is the integration layer — and they're exhausted. |

## What This Costs You — Measured and Hidden

### Measurable Monthly Losses

| Category | Monthly Cost | How It's Calculated |
|----------|:------------:|---------------------|
| Admin labour wasted on manual tasks | R32,500 | 6.5 hours/day × R250/hour × 20 working days. Your team spends over a full workday every day on tasks that should take minutes. |
| Revenue lost to patient no-shows | R45,000 | ~20% no-show rate × R1,500 average appointment value × ~150 monthly appointments. Nearly one in five appointments simply doesn't show up — and you have no automated way to prevent it. |
| Marketing labour (manual content creation) | R8,000+ | 4+ hours/day creating social media content, with no strategy engine or scheduling tool. |
| Medical aid verification time | R2,500+ | 1+ hour/day of staff time calling medical aids to verify benefits. |
| **Total measurable monthly loss** | **R88,000+** | **Over R1,000,000 per year.** |

### Hidden Costs — The Ones You Can't See on a Spreadsheet

| Hidden Cost | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **After-hours enquiries** | Patients who reach out after 5pm or on weekends get no response. By Monday morning, many have already booked with a competitor. You have zero visibility into how many enquiries you're losing. |
| **Inconsistent follow-up** | Aesthetics patients who don't rebook within 7 days are 60% less likely to return. Without automated follow-up sequences, you're relying on memory and good intentions — and both fail. |
| **Zero visibility** | You cannot see your revenue per doctor, per procedure, or per day in real time. Decisions are made on instinct rather than data. You discover problems weeks after they start. |
| **Paper risk** | A single fire, flood, or misfiled folder could compromise years of patient records. POPIA requires digital records with proper access controls. Paper files provide neither. |
| **Staff burnout** | Your team of 14 is carrying a workload designed for 20+. Manual processes create bottlenecks, errors, and frustration. Your best people are spending their talent on data entry instead of patient care. |
| **Triple data entry** | Every appointment, every invoice, every patient record is entered 2–3 times across different systems. This isn't just slow — it creates discrepancies that take even more time to resolve. |
| **Missed product revenue** | LAISA patients who buy skincare products in-clinic have no automated reorder reminders. A simple follow-up sequence could increase product revenue by 20–30%. |
| **Competitor advancement** | Every month you operate manually, digitally-enabled clinics are capturing patients who search online, book instantly, and receive automated follow-up — patients who should be yours. |

## Two Businesses, Two Sets of Problems

> *SafeSight and LAISA share a practice, but they face fundamentally different challenges. Treating them as one problem obscures what each business actually needs. Here is each business's specific pain — in plain language.*

### SafeSight-Specific Problems (Eye Surgery)

| # | Problem | Why It Hurts SafeSight Specifically |
|---|---------|--------------------------------------|
| 1 | **Referral leakage** | Ophthalmology patients come through GP and optometrist referrals. Without a digital referral pathway, referring doctors send patients to competitors who are easier to find online. SafeSight has no system to receive, track, or follow up on referrals. |
| 2 | **Surgical conversion bottleneck** | Not every consultation becomes surgery. Currently, there is no system to track consultation-to-surgery conversion, identify drop-off points, or nurture patients who need time to decide on surgical procedures worth R8K–R50K+. |
| 3 | **Medical aid complexity** | Medical aid pre-authorisation requires phone calls, document submission, and follow-up. Each claim takes 15–20 minutes of staff time. Wrong codes delay payment by weeks. There is no automated verification or claims tracking. |
| 4 | **No surgical funnel visibility** | The practice cannot see how many consultations led to surgeries, which procedures generate the most revenue, or where patients drop off between diagnosis and surgical booking. |
| 5 | **Post-operative follow-up gaps** | Cataract and retinal surgery patients require structured follow-up schedules. Currently, if a patient does not book their post-op visit at checkout, there is no automated recall system. Missed post-op appointments can have serious clinical consequences. |
| 6 | **Clinical authority not visible online** | Patients choosing an eye surgeon research credentials, outcomes, and specialist expertise online. SafeSight's current web presence does not communicate surgical authority or build the trust required for someone to entrust their vision to a practice. |

### LAISA-Specific Problems (Aesthetics)

| # | Problem | Why It Hurts LAISA Specifically |
|---|---------|--------------------------------------|
| 1 | **Instagram discovery gap** | Aesthetics patients discover providers through Instagram, TikTok, and before-and-after content. LAISA's social media is created manually with no strategy, no scheduling, and no measurement. 4+ hours/day of someone's time for content that is inconsistent and unmeasured. |
| 2 | **No rebooking engine** | Aesthetics is a repeat-visit business. Botox needs touch-ups every 3–4 months. Fillers every 6–12 months. Skincare requires ongoing treatment plans. Without automated rebooking reminders, patients simply forget to come back. The #1 reason patients do not return is that nobody reminded them. |
| 3 | **Product revenue left on the table** | LAISA patients buy skincare products in-clinic, but there is no product catalogue system, no automated reorder reminders, and no follow-up sequence to drive product repurchases. A simple "Your moisturiser is running low" message could increase product revenue by 20–30%. |
| 4 | **Aesthetic consultation drop-off** | Patients who enquire about Botox, fillers, or skin treatments are often making an emotional decision. If they do not receive a response within minutes — not hours — they book elsewhere. After-hours DMs and WhatsApp messages sit unanswered until morning, by which point the patient has already committed to a competitor. |
| 5 | **Before-and-after content not systemised** | Visual results sell aesthetic treatments. Currently, there is no system to capture before-and-after photos consistently, tag them by treatment type, and deploy them as social proof across marketing channels. This is the single most powerful content type for an aesthetics practice, and it is entirely ad hoc. |
| 6 | **No seasonal or event-based marketing** | Aesthetics has predictable demand cycles: December holidays, Valentine's Day, wedding season, summer. Without automated campaigns targeting these cycles, LAISA misses revenue spikes that competitors capture. |

## What Patients Expect in 2026

The patients walking through your door in 2026 have different expectations than they did even two years ago. They order groceries on their phones. They book haircuts online at 11pm. They expect confirmation within seconds, not hours. They expect reminders, not phone tag. They expect to feel taken care of before they arrive, not just after.

### SafeSight Patient Expectations (Eye Surgery)

| Patient Expectation | Reality at SafeSight |
|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| "I should be able to book online at any hour." | Only phone and WhatsApp during business hours. |
| "I should get an instant confirmation." | Confirmation depends on staff availability. |
| "My referring doctor should be able to send you my records digitally." | No digital referral pathway. Referrals come by fax or phone. |
| "My medical aid should be verified before I arrive." | Staff calls medical aids manually — 15–20 min per call. |
| "I should get a reminder the day before surgery." | Reminders are sent manually, and sometimes forgotten. |
| "My records should be secure and digital." | Paper files in cabinets, some on Google Drive. |
| "I should be able to ask questions after hours." | No after-hours response. Messages queue until morning. |
| "The practice should follow up with me after surgery." | Follow-up depends on whether someone remembers to call. |
