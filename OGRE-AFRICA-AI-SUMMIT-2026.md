## THE OPPORTUNITY

Africa has the world's youngest population — 600 million people under 25. By 2050, Africa will have 1 in 3 of the world's working-age people. Yet less than 1% of the world's AI research comes from Africa.

The gap is not talent. The gap is **infrastructure and access.**

OGRE's Youth Team Programme bridges that gap by:
1. Building real AI infrastructure in African cities
2. Training young Africans to operate it
3. Employing them to run it
4. Creating a self-sustaining sovereign AI workforce

## 3-MONTH TRAINING CURRICULUM

### Month 1 — Infrastructure Fundamentals
**Week 1-2:** Linux command line, networking fundamentals, DNS, SSH
**Week 3-4:** Data centre operations (ART MODULAR hardware) — power, cooling, physical security
**Assessment:** CompTIA A+ equivalent lab exam

### Month 2 — AI Platform Operations
**Week 1-2:** OGRE VM platform — provisioning, management, monitoring
**Week 3-4:** AI agents — n8n automation, Claude Code, prompt engineering
**Assessment:** Deploy a working AI agent for a simulated client

### Month 3 — Real World Project
**Week 1-2:** Client project with a real African business (partner with local SME)
**Week 3-4:** Red Team Agent security audit + compliance documentation
**Final Assessment:** Present project to panel + receive certification

### Certifications Earned:
- **OGRE Certified AI Technician (Level 1)** — issued by Dark Factory
- **ART MODULAR Data Centre Operations (Level 1)** — issued by ART Engineering
- **n8n Automation Associate** — issued by OGRE Academy

## FUNDING MODEL

Each 20-student cohort costs approximately **$15,000** to train:

| Cost Item | Cost |
|-----------|------|
| Instructors (3 months) | $6,000 |
| Equipment (shared, per cohort) | $4,000 |
| Certification | $1,500 |
| Transport + venue | $2,000 |
| Stipend for students | $1,500 |
| **Total per cohort** | **$15,000** |

**Funding sources:**
- 50% from DC revenue (the DC the cohort manages generates enough in 1 month to fund the next cohort)
- 25% from government skills levies
- 25% from impact investors (Alami Capital, AfDB)

**Self-sustaining by Year 2:** Each city's DC covers its own youth programme costs from month 1.
