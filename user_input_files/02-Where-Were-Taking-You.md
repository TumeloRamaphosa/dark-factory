## Two Businesses, One Transformation

> *SafeSight and LAISA share a practice, but they serve completely different patients in completely different markets. The transformation must reflect this reality: two distinct brands, two distinct digital presences, two distinct patient experiences — all powered by one unified backend.*

### The Principle: Separate Out Front, Unified Behind the Scenes

```
PATIENT-FACING (Separate)                    BACKEND (Shared)

safesight.co.za          laisa.aesthetic      Patient Records (tagged by business)
    |                         |               Billing & Accounting (Sage)
    v                         v               Scheduling System
SafeSight WhatsApp      LAISA WhatsApp        AI Agent Team (16 assistants)
Professional,           Warm, personal,        Knowledge Base
medical tone            beauty-focused tone     Compliance (POPIA)
    |                         |               Reporting Dashboard
    v                         v
Eye surgery patients    Aesthetics patients    Revenue split by business
```

### Why Separation Matters

- **SafeSight patients** are making a medical decision about their vision. They need clinical authority, surgical credentials, and a reassuring, professional experience. They arrive through referrals and medical aid directories. They are comparing you to Vision Works, Optimed, and Spec-Savers.

- **LAISA patients** are making an aesthetic choice about their appearance. They need visual inspiration, social proof, and a warm, aspirational experience. They arrive through Instagram and word of mouth. They are comparing you to Skin & Body Renewal, LaserAway, and aesthetic medispas.

- **A merged brand** serves neither patient well. An eye surgery patient does not want to see Botox promotions. An aesthetics patient does not want to read about glaucoma treatment. The transformation builds two brands that speak to two audiences — while sharing the operational backbone that makes both efficient.
